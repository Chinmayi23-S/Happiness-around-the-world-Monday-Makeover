# Happiness-around-the-world-Monday-Makeover
Happiness around the world varies greatly, shaped by the psychological and social factors that influence individuals well being.

The six major factors commonly used in the World Happiness Report to explain differences in happiness between countries are:

**1. GDP per Capita (Income)**
Measures average income and living standards. Higher income generally gives people better access to necessities, healthcare, and opportunities.
**2. Social Support**
Reflects whether people have family, friends, or community members they can rely on during difficult times.
**3. Healthy Life Expectancy**
Measures how long people can expect to live in good health. Better health often leads to greater well-being and life satisfaction.
**4. Freedom to Make Life Choices**
Indicates how much control people feel they have over important decisions in their lives, such as work, education, and lifestyle.
**5. Generosity**
Measures willingness to help others, donate, or engage in acts of kindness. Societies with higher generosity often report higher happiness.
**6. Perceptions of Corruption**
Reflects trust in government and institutions. Lower levels of perceived corruption are generally linked to higher happiness.


### Key Insights

**Finland** stands out as the <u>happiest country</u>, with a score **7.187**, followed by **Denmark** and **Iceland**. 
These nations show how Cultural stability, Economic growth, and strong Social support foster level of happiness .

**Venezuela's** happiness heavily depends on cultural and some unexplained elements while Economic growth contributes little to its overall well-being.

**Sri Lanka** shows almost no cultural influence on the individuals, suggesting measurable factors dominate its profile.

Interestingly, <u>Economic growth</u> of both **Singapore and Ireland** are the <u>highest</u>, yet this example proves wealth alone does not guarantee happiness.

In Contrast, **Afghanistan** ranks as <u>least</u> happy country highlighting severe lack of basic needs and resources available to its people.

**Lebanon** is the only country with <u>negative residual score</u>. Ranking close to Afghanistan, highlight the cultural & societal struggles of the people that cannot explained by 6 Globally recognize factors alone.

Ultimately , while these comparisons provide a global perspective, happiness is deeply personal - meaning that even in Finland not every individual experiences happiness in the same way. It depends not only on economic and social indicators but also on individual mindset, perception, and countless other influences beyond standardized measures.
